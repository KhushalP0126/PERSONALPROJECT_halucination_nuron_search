Milestone report
Date: 2026-03-29

Summary

This project now has a real, minimal result.

The old locked baseline used a fixed `late_slope` over the last 5 layers and reached:
- Dev accuracy: 0.576
- Holdout accuracy: 0.580

The upgraded single-feature signal uses `late_window_slope`, defined as the slope over the final 30% of support layers. That improves to:
- Dev accuracy: 0.657
- Holdout accuracy: 0.620
- Holdout ROC AUC: 0.678

The best minimal system combines:
- `late_window_slope`
- `logit_confidence`

This 2-feature logistic regression is trained on development only and evaluated unchanged on holdout:
- Dev accuracy: 0.606
- Holdout accuracy: 0.640
- Dev ROC AUC: 0.677
- Holdout ROC AUC: 0.703

Core result

Late-layer convergence is a weak standalone signal, but it becomes a useful predictor when combined with model confidence.

The cleanest statement of the result is:

"Late-layer convergence provides complementary information to model confidence and improves correctness prediction."

Why this matters

1. The signal is real
- Moving from the old fixed-window slope to `late_window_slope` raises holdout accuracy from 0.580 to 0.620.
- That means late-stage behavior matters, and the measurement choice is important.

2. The combination works
- The combined model improves holdout accuracy again, from 0.620 to 0.640.
- The holdout ROC AUC of 0.703 is the best result in the current system.

3. The signals are complementary
- corr(`late_window_slope`, `logit_confidence`) on dev: -0.0558
- corr(`late_window_slope`, `logit_confidence`) on holdout: -0.0792
- These are effectively zero, so the two features are not just duplicates of the same signal.

4. The failures are interpretable
- High-score wrong cases are often confident misconceptions or stereotype-like yes/no claims.
- Low-score correct cases are often weakly phrased, benchmark-edge, or ambiguous-label cases.
- This suggests `late_window_slope` tracks internal convergence or coherence better than it tracks ground truth directly.

Locked setup

Frozen internal feature
- `late_window_slope`
- Definition: slope of a first-order fit over the final 30% of support layers
- Interpretation: higher is more likely to be correct

Frozen minimal classifier
- Logistic regression on `late_window_slope` and `logit_confidence`
- Fit on development only
- Applied unchanged to holdout

Interpretation

This is not a production-ready detector and not a state-of-the-art result. It is a real, interpretable, generalizing signal with a minimal system around it.

The project has crossed from "interesting signal" to "usable result":
- the signal survives holdout evaluation
- the combined model improves the baseline
- the feature interaction is conceptually clean
- the failure modes are understandable
